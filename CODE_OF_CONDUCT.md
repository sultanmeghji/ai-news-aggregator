# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We are committed to providing a friendly, safe and welcoming environment for all contributors and users of the AI News Aggregator project, regardless of experience level, gender identity and expression, sexual orientation, disability, personal appearance, body size, race, ethnicity, age, religion, nationality, or other similar characteristics.

## Our Standards

Examples of behavior that contributes to creating a positive environment include:

### Positive Behaviors
- **Being respectful and inclusive** in all interactions
- **Constructive feedback** that focuses on improvement
- **Helping others** learn and grow
- **Acknowledging contributions** from all community members
- **Focusing on collaboration** rather than competition
- **Being patient** with newcomers and different skill levels
- **Gracefully accepting** constructive criticism

### Unacceptable Behaviors
- **Harassment or discrimination** of any kind
- **Trolling, insulting, or derogatory comments**
- **Personal or political attacks**
- **Public or private harassment**
- **Publishing others' private information** without permission
- **Inappropriate sexual language or imagery**
- **Other conduct** that could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional setting

## Our Responsibilities

Project maintainers are responsible for clarifying the standards of acceptable behavior and are expected to take appropriate and fair corrective action in response to any instances of unacceptable behavior.

Maintainers have the right and responsibility to remove, edit, or reject:
- Comments, commits, code, wiki edits, issues, and other contributions that are not aligned with this Code of Conduct
- Contributors who engage in inappropriate behavior

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to all project spaces, including:
- GitHub repositories and discussions
- Issue trackers and pull requests
- Documentation and wikis
- Community forums or chat rooms
- Social media interactions related to the project
- In-person events and meetups

## Enforcement

### Reporting
If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, or have any other concerns, please report it by:
- Opening a private issue on GitHub
- Contacting project maintainers directly
- Using GitHub's reporting features

All complaints will be reviewed and investigated promptly and fairly.

### Response
Project maintainers will respond to reports by:
1. **Acknowledging the report** within 48 hours
2. **Investigating the situation** fairly and thoroughly
3. **Taking appropriate action** based on the severity
4. **Following up** with all parties involved

### Consequences
Depending on the severity and nature of the violation, consequences may include:

#### 1. Correction
**Community Impact**: Use of inappropriate language or other behavior deemed unprofessional or unwelcome.

**Consequence**: A private, written warning from maintainers, providing clarity around the nature of the violation and an explanation of why the behavior was inappropriate.

#### 2. Warning
**Community Impact**: A violation through a single incident or series of actions.

**Consequence**: A warning with consequences for continued behavior. No interaction with the people involved for a specified period of time.

#### 3. Temporary Ban
**Community Impact**: A serious violation of community standards, including sustained inappropriate behavior.

**Consequence**: A temporary ban from any sort of interaction or public communication with the project community for a specified period of time.

#### 4. Permanent Ban
**Community Impact**: Demonstrating a pattern of violation of community standards, including sustained inappropriate behavior, harassment, or aggression.

**Consequence**: A permanent ban from any sort of public interaction within the project community.

## Guidelines for Interactions

### For All Community Members
- **Be welcoming** to newcomers
- **Assume good intentions** in interactions
- **Ask questions** when you don't understand something
- **Offer help** when you can
- **Give credit** where it's due
- **Be patient** with different skill levels

### For Contributors
- **Review code constructively** with specific, actionable feedback
- **Explain your reasoning** when suggesting changes
- **Be open to feedback** on your own contributions
- **Help maintain documentation** and examples
- **Follow project guidelines** and conventions

### For Maintainers
- **Provide clear guidance** on contribution expectations
- **Review contributions fairly** and promptly
- **Communicate decisions** clearly and respectfully
- **Recognize and celebrate** community contributions
- **Foster an inclusive environment** for all participants

## Building a Positive Community

### Encouraging Participation
- **Welcome new contributors** with helpful onboarding
- **Provide clear documentation** and examples
- **Offer mentorship** opportunities
- **Celebrate diverse perspectives** and approaches
- **Create opportunities** for community engagement

### Fostering Learning
- **Share knowledge** through documentation and discussions
- **Provide constructive feedback** on code and ideas
- **Encourage experimentation** and innovation
- **Support skill development** at all levels
- **Create safe spaces** for asking questions

### Maintaining Quality
- **Establish clear standards** for code and documentation
- **Provide helpful tools** and resources
- **Maintain consistent processes** for contributions
- **Balance quality with accessibility** for new contributors
- **Regularly review and improve** project practices

## Resources and Support

### Getting Help
- **Documentation**: Check README.md and CONTRIBUTING.md
- **Issues**: Search existing issues before creating new ones
- **Discussions**: Use GitHub discussions for questions
- **Community**: Engage respectfully with other contributors

### Conflict Resolution
If you're experiencing a conflict:
1. **Try direct communication** first, if appropriate
2. **Ask for help** from maintainers if needed
3. **Use the reporting process** for serious issues
4. **Focus on resolution** rather than blame

### Support Resources
- **Project Documentation**: README.md, CONTRIBUTING.md
- **Community Guidelines**: This Code of Conduct
- **Technical Support**: GitHub Issues and Discussions
- **Reporting Issues**: Private contact with maintainers
